My Personal Code of Ethics

My Career Aspirations

In the future, I hope to become a Machine Learning Engineer (MLE) specializing in creating ethical AI applications that make of others' lives more convenient while also ensuring public safety. I hope to pursue this career path because it combines my programming aptness with my desire for positively impacting society. My motivation for this job is inspired by past experiences, such as developing a machine learning plant disease detection app and contributing to global health initiatives like MedShare. Through this career, I aim to address diverse challenges, from building advanced disease detection systems to designing practical tools that simplify everyday tasks.

Ethical Principles

Integrity

Integrity is the cornerstone of my ethical framework. I will uphold honesty and transparency in every aspect of my work, ensuring that stakeholders are well-informed about the biases or limitations in my models.

  • Honesty: I define honesty as being truthful in all my communications and actions. For instance, I will accurately report project outcomes and methodologies without exaggerating or misrepresenting results.
  • Transparency: Transparency involves openly sharing the processes, limitations, and potential implications of my work. I will provide clear documentation about how data is stored, processed, and used, as well as detailed explanations of my algorithm development processes when publishing code.

Commitment to the Common Good

I am committed to developing software and technologies that positively contribute to the common good. This means carefully evaluating the societal impact of my projects and striving to create solutions that benefit all individuals equitably.

  • Definition: The "common good" refers to solutions that generate positive outcomes for society as a whole, addressing needs while minimizing harm.
  • I will proactively identify and mitigate any biases in my projects that could lead to discrimination based on race, gender, or other protected categories.
  • Respecting user privacy is paramount. I will ensure that all data is anonymized, securely stored, and handled in compliance with regulations such as GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) and HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act).

Accountability

Accountability means taking full responsibility for my creations and being receptive to constructive feedback to improve my practices.

  • I will establish clear mechanisms for monitoring the performance and ethical implications of my software after deployment.
  • When mistakes or unintended consequences arise, I will take immediate action to address and rectify the issues, ensuring harm is minimized.

Evolution of My Code of Ethics

As I advance in my career, I understand that my ethical framework must evolve to address new challenges and technological advancements. My participation in courses like E125 has taught me the profound impact small decisions can have, often creating cascading effects. For example, initial missteps, such as those seen in the Flint Water Crisis, illustrate how seemingly minor choices can grow to have widespread consequences. I carry this mindset into my work, continuously refining my ethical principles to ensure that I am prepared to tackle emerging dilemmas.

Additionally, my volunteer experience with Medshare and participation in biomedical research competitions have deepened my appreciation for ethical responsibility in both professional and personal endeavors. These experiences remind me of the importance of empathy and collaboration in designing solutions that serve diverse communities effectively.
